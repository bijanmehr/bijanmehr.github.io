Project Summary

My Human-Robot Interaction portfolio unites large-scale, user-centered systems for diagnostic assessment, emotional learning, and rehabilitative therapy, all designed to foster intuitive and meaningful user engagement.

PlaySense

Autism Screening Experience

A multi-robot ecosystem that pairs sensorized toys (wheel encoders + IMU) with a mobile robot equipped with RGB cameras. I designed and implemented the end-to-end data pipeline: Python-based vision and behavior-analysis algorithms, synchronized data logging, and multi-modal signal fusion. This integrated approach surpassed single-sensor methods in early ASD detection (ICRoM 2023; Sustainability 2023).

Emotional Learning Experience

A “Robotic Social Environment” where expressive robots enact emotion-driven narratives, prompting children to observe and name feelings. I developed the synchronization engine aligning speech, gesture, and visual prompts, and integrated MediaPipe-based facial tracking to adapt interactions in real time. A Frontiers in Robotics and AI study showed that participants retained these emotional-recognition skills weeks after sessions, building on earlier work presented at ICSR 2020.

Rehabilitative Platform: HandMotion+

In HandMotion+, our hand-therapy platform, palm stiffness is inferred directly from motor current via an impedance-control observer—no external force sensors needed. I implemented the sensor-fusion algorithms and crafted a Python-based, joystick-style gamification interface that turns therapeutic exercises into motivating, game-like tasks while logging performance metrics for adaptive therapy planning. This platform is a key part of an R&D program aimed at democratizing robotic rehabilitative services.
